Ed Sheeran

  • February 17, 1991 - Edward Christopher Sheeran was born
  • 2005 - Recoded his first album "Spinning man" with some cash he borrowed and scraped together. He sold all 20 copies but now has all but 1 copy and says he doesn't want people to listen to them
  • March 2005 - Finished his second album titled "The Orange Room EP"
  • March 22, 2006 - Released his self-titled album "Ed Sheeran"
  • June 29, 2007 - Released the EP "Want Some?"
  • 2008 - Ed moved to London based on some advice from his dad and the inspiration of Ed's idol, Damien Rice
  • 2008+ - Ed Sheeran began gigging regularly around London's local pubs, bars, and clubs
  • November 2, 2009 - Released the Ep "You Need Me, I Don't Need You"
  • February 7, 2010 - Released EP titled "Loose Change"
  • February 22, 2010 - SBTV did an episode of Ed in-studio with Jake Gosling standing nearby while recording Ed's loop pedal version of his crash-course-styled song, "You Need Me, I Don't Need You" in 1 take, live
  • April 4, 2010 - Ed released another EP titled "Songs I Wrote With Amy"
  • September 7, 2010 - Released the EP "Live At The Bedford" which was a live recording of a performance that was a bit different for Ed, having a full experienced band to back him
  • January 8, 2011 - Ed released his EP titled "No. 5 Collaborations" which features many rappers from the London-founded genre of rap called "grime". It was his most proud achievement to date and he really liked how it turned out
  • January 24, 2011 - Ed released his first single after having singed with Atlantic Records, his first record deal. The single's name was "A Team"
  • April 7, 2011 - Ed recorded his 3-track album with Atlantic which was what it is, a "One Take EP"
  • September 9, 2011 - The culmination of his efforts had given way and Atlantic released Ed's debut studio album, "+" (pronounced "plus")
  • February 14, 2012 - Released another grime EP, this time with rapper Yelawolf titled "Slumdon Bridge"
  • June 23, 2014 - The second official album by Ed and his team at Atlantic: "x" (pronounced "multiply")
  • March 3, 2017 - Ed released his third album after he had seemed to disappear for a whole year and named it "รท" (pronounced "divide")
